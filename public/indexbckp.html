About Us

Alpha Psi Lambda is the nation's First and Largest Co-ed Latino oriented Social Greek Fraternity. We are a family-based organization that breaks through stereotypical boundaries and eases its way into a plethora of cultures. We are a unique Greek Fraternity where only the Noblest Brothers and Sisters come together to form the great family that Alpha Psi Lambda represents.

Chapter History

One night in October of 1991 at the UIC's Rafael Cintron Cultural Center, Eliezer Perez and Derrick Cabrera were talking about creating an organization. Saul Garcia, who is one of the Alpha Psi Lambda founders at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, began talks with both of them about the fraternity. They spoke about its history, community service, and that the fraternity being co-ed will be a learning experience. However, the family aspect of the fraternity caught the attention of the two students. After some research, they decided to start a new chapter of Alpha Psi Lambda at University of Illinois at Chicago.

Subsequently, they consulted the Northern Illinois University Chapter, and began recruiting people who were interested in establishing a new chapter. From the informational meetings, twenty one interested students were recruited, and on October 17, 1992 approval was given to NIU to charter the prospective group which had dropped to fifteen.

On April 24, 1993 at 3:24 AM, after being on line for 155 days the twelve remaining students crossed over and the Alpha Psi Lambda, Zeta Chapter was Founded.
